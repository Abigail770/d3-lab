Living Conditions in Spain

A look into homelessness in 2018


Even since recovering from the 2008 recession, homelessness remains a prevalent issue in Spain. Part of this problem is due to a lack of public housing, stemming from the 1960's with government tax breaks for homeowners - as a result, little public housing was constructed. Now, many empty privately-owned housing units have become home to squatters.



Overall, Spain enjoys a relatively high standard of living compared to many other countries. However, there is a large income gap between the wealthy and those in poverty: the top 20% earn almost 7 times that of the poorest 20%.



Although the homeless population continues to rise in Spain, efforts have been made to expand resources like shelters. According to a study conducted by Pompeu Fabra University in Barcelona, the capacity of the housing network for homeless people increased from 13,033 in 2006 to 16,346 in 2012.
